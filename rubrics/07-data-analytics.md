# Dimension 7: Data & Analytics

**Composite Weight: 5%**

## Description

Examines how well the team has thought through the data requirements, quality
considerations, governance frameworks, and analytics capabilities needed to support
their proposed technology initiative. Modern digital transformation efforts are
fundamentally data-driven, and executives expect teams to demonstrate that they have
considered how data will be sourced, managed, governed, and leveraged for insight.

## Related Personas

- **CDO** - Evaluates data strategy, governance, quality, and analytics maturity

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Data Requirements | 25% | Has the team identified what data is needed, where it comes from, and how it will be acquired or integrated? Are data volume and velocity considered? |
| 2 | Data Quality | 20% | Has the team addressed data quality challenges (completeness, accuracy, consistency, timeliness)? Are data cleansing or validation steps planned? |
| 3 | Data Governance | 20% | Is there a plan for data ownership, stewardship, lineage, and lifecycle management? Are governance policies identified? |
| 4 | Analytics Capability | 20% | Has the team considered what analytics capabilities are needed (descriptive, predictive, prescriptive)? Are tools and skills requirements identified? |
| 5 | Ethical Considerations | 15% | Has the team addressed ethical dimensions of data use including bias, fairness, transparency, and responsible AI/ML practices? |

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Not Addressed | Topic not mentioned or considered |
| 2 | Superficial | Acknowledged but lacks depth or evidence |
| 3 | Adequate | Reasonable treatment with some supporting evidence |
| 4 | Strong | Well-developed with evidence, frameworks, and analysis |
| 5 | Exceptional | Comprehensive, nuanced, with creative insights and strong evidence |
