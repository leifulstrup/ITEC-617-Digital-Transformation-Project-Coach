# Dimension 6: Change Management & People

**Composite Weight: 10%**

## Description

Evaluates whether the team has considered the human side of their proposed digital
transformation initiative. Technology implementations frequently fail not because of
technical shortcomings but because of insufficient attention to organizational change.
This dimension examines stakeholder engagement, workforce readiness, communication
planning, and cultural alignment. Executives know that people challenges are often harder
than technology challenges.

## Related Personas

- **CHRO** - Evaluates workforce readiness, training plans, and cultural impact
- **COO** - Assesses operational change management and stakeholder engagement

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Stakeholder Analysis | 20% | Has the team identified key stakeholders, their interests, influence, and potential resistance? Is there a stakeholder engagement plan? |
| 2 | Change Readiness | 20% | Has the team assessed the organization's readiness for the proposed change? Are change readiness factors (culture, capacity, history) considered? |
| 3 | Training Plan | 20% | Is there a plan for upskilling or reskilling affected employees? Are training methods, timelines, and resource needs identified? |
| 4 | Communication Strategy | 20% | Has the team planned how to communicate the change to different audiences? Are messaging, channels, and timing addressed? |
| 5 | Cultural Considerations | 20% | Does the team recognize how organizational culture may support or hinder adoption? Are cultural alignment strategies proposed? |
